## Finding a Project Topic

Went through a list of proposals we each came up with beforehand:

- ~~Proposal 1 — AI Study Assistant~~ → weak system scope, basically a chatbot wrapper
- ~~Proposal 2 — AI Knowledge Base (RAG System)~~ → interesting technically, but no clear application domain / user story
- **Proposal 3 — AI DevOps Incident Assistant** → picked this one (Leon's idea — he encountered this kind of tooling during a deployment at a company)
- ~~Proposal 4 — AI Meal Planner & Grocery Optimizer~~ → GenAI angle fine, but domain felt arbitrary
- ~~Proposal 5 — AI Travel Planner~~ → same issue, doesn't connect to the course theme

Landed on **Proposal 3** fairly quickly once we talked through it:

- fits the team name (panic at the console)
- real GenAI use case: summarize incident context, suggest severity, draft postmortems
- event-driven architecture is natural here → good fit for microservices
- connects directly to the DevOps theme of the course
- CI webhook integration makes for a clean demo story (pipeline fails → incident auto-created)

Manuel sketched a rough service breakdown using ungodly amounts of LLM calls, Florian wrote things down and helped break the system into concrete subtasks and microservices, Leon started thinking about what the AI service would actually do.

Main concern raised: is this too complex for 3 people? → Agreed to keep the core small (incident service + event log + frontend) and treat the rest as extensions.

**Key principle we agreed on:** GenAI must be a real feature, not decoration. It reads the event log and incident metadata and produces something useful (summary, triage, postmortem draft). Not just "ask ChatGPT".
